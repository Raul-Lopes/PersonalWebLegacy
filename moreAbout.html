Living An Irish Life

previous next

After years of dreaming about doing an interchange, I finally put into practice a dream I had since the beginning of my adolescence. I resigned from my "old" job, took some money I have saved. I gave my mother a hug, and I went ahead "trying to get luck". To be honest I have chosen Ireland but "it was not my favorite cup of tea". I chose the country because it is a cheaper destination for those who intend to live abroad and still be able to study the English language and be able to work at the same time if necessary.

I am living in the city of Cork, the second largest city in the country, since the beginning of May 2017. From there my life is lived with great intensity and many things are still new to me, even after over than a year. A new life, in a new country, with a new language and new friends, fantastic! Ireland is incredible. I have not liked the country at first, but I learned to enjoy living here. How can a small town, where I live, have people from different parts of the world? The Irish are fantastic too. They are very lively and you do not think it strange if some of them talk to you in the streets or in a pub. By speaking in pub, it is almost impossible to walk the streets of Cork without crossing for one.

Ireland's weather is very dynamic, but it's something you learn to live with. you learn that umbrella does not make much effect and that a sunny morning does not necessarily mean that the day is going to be sunny. Rain, snow, sun, gale. It is normal to do all this in one day.

Great friendships are being made at school, but the bad news is that everyone is here only for a short time. At the same time that everything is very intense, everything ends very quickly. It is common to cling to friends who live with you at school and after school. When you realize, there are no longer any of them here after a few months.

Galway, Ring of Kerry, Cliffs of Moher and Dublin

I've done some road trip by car and I've been able to meet some cities as Dublin or counties like Limerick, Kirlarnney and Galway. Some cities look like toys, with colorful houses, clean streets and a cozy climate. I have made a stop at the Cliffs of Moher, Ireland's main postcard and one of the most beautiful scenery I have ever seen in my life.

My greatest learning to date is leaving a comfort zone every day. I'd always heard of it, but it was impossible to feel unless you did something to really make it happen. Of course, fear exists! but standing stoped is a risk as well.

Living outside you learn to be more economical, you learn to do, and, what you will have to do, things that others have been done for you. You learn a new language and you live your life speaking this new language, do not matter how much it is complicated. You learn that it will be you who will wash your clothes, you will do your shopping and your food.

Well, I did not have much difficulty with that since I've been trained at home, but a lot of people can not stand to stay away from the family and the pampering they have. Many give up in the first months of interchange, returning before the course ends. But just like those who give up, others get on super good and stay for years away. I hope to live here for many, many years.
